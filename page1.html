Sailor Moon

Sailor Moon, created in 1991 by Naoko Takeuchi, is a magical girl manga and anime. All of the heroes in this series are mainly female protagonists and have transformation sequences for their respective planetary signs. The Sailor Guardians each have unique abilities and ultimately fight together to ward away evil. Their main goals are protecting the Earth and the Silver Crystal. Additionally, it is one of the first magical girl animes to have uncensored LGBTQ+ representation. Viewers and readers follow Usagi, the teenage girl protagonist through the discovery of her powers and the formation of her friend group, the Sailor Scouts.

Impact

The creation of Sailor Moon has shown its impact through the influence it had on the genre and media itself. Lots of moments in pop culture can be observed in the stylization and development of this manga/anime. Sailor Moon being one of the most recognizable animes and being one of the pioneers for the magical girl genre has allowed it to inspire and positively impact audiences. Not only does it serve as artistic inspiration, but shares valuable lessons through it's coming of age story. It's noted as many people's first anime and spans multiple series, movies, and books.
